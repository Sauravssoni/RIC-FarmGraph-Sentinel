# FarmGraph Rakshak — Indicative Grant Use Plan

**Proposed milestone-based request:** **₹2.95 crore over 24 months**  
**Applicant:** Syntheon Technology Private Limited  
**Status:** Indicative proposal subject to Rajasthan Innovation Challenge terms, government-approved scope and procurement rules.

## Funding principle

The request is deliberately staged. Rajasthan should not fund a state-scale rollout before the 90-day pilot proves evidence quality, operational SLAs, safety, model evaluation and institutional adoption.

| Stage | Period | Indicative amount | Decision gate |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Stage 1 — Three-district validation | Months 0–3 | ₹48.60 lakh | Proceed only if pilot safety, evidence, response and usability gates pass |
| Stage 2 — Six-district hardening | Months 4–12 | ₹1.16 crore | Proceed only if model, integration, cost and programme acceptance gates pass |
| Stage 3 — State-scale readiness | Months 13–24 | ₹1.304 crore | Production-readiness, government handoff and approved phased expansion |
| **Total** | **24 months** | **₹2.95 crore** | Milestone controlled |

## Stage 1 — Three-district validation: ₹48.60 lakh

- validate the field-to-command-centre workflow;
- curate and evaluate Rajasthan evidence;
- test KVK and expert response;
- measure safety, comprehension and outcome follow-up;

## Grant safeguards

- Milestone acceptance before tranche release.
- Government ownership/usage rights defined before production work.
- No claim of model accuracy without the approved evaluation dataset.
- No automatic model promotion.
- No farmer data sale or advertising model.
- No dealer commission influencing agronomic advice.
- Chemical guidance remains approval-gated.
- Government APIs used only under applicable terms and authority.
- Open standards and exportable evidence reduce vendor lock-in.
- All material subcontracting and procurement disclosed.

## Co-funding and sustainability

Syntheon will contribute existing prototype IP, engineering assets, documentation and founder/technical effort. Additional co-funding, cloud credits, institutional resources or field infrastructure may reduce the grant requirement, but no unconfirmed contribution is included as committed funding.

Post-grant sustainability may combine:

- state programme operations;
- approved annual platform/support contract;
- implementation and integration services;
- KVK/FPO/cooperative programme deployments;
- government-approved model and data-governance services.

Essential farmer reporting and advisory access should remain free in government-funded programmes.
